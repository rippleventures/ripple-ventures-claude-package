# Predatory terms checklist

Always scan a term sheet, SAFE, or side letter for these patterns. They're the things aggressive counterparties try to slip in hoping no one will notice.

## Economics

**Multiple liquidation preference (above 1x).** Anything above 1x is a red flag at seed/Series A.
- 2x is aggressive in the US, off-market in Canada
- 3x+ is predatory
- 10x is "you tried to steal the company on a down exit"

Always state the dollar implication. At a $50M exit with a $10M investment at 3x non-participating, the investor takes $30M off the top before founders see a cent.

**Counter:** 1x non-participating preferred is standard.

---

**Participating preferred.** Investor takes their preference AND shares pro rata in the rest. Off-market at seed in Canada. Even at later stages, ask for a cap (typically 2x or 3x).

**Counter:** Non-participating preferred. If participating, cap at 2x.

---

**Cumulative dividends.** Creates a ratcheting preference over time. Not standard for venture. PE-style.

**Counter:** Non-cumulative dividends, typically 6-8% as-declared. Or no dividend rights at all.

---

**Mandatory redemption.** Debt-like obligation. Not in CVCA standard forms. Watch for short windows (under 5 years) or redemption at original price + premium.

**Counter:** Strike redemption rights. If the lead insists, push for ≥7 year window and at original purchase price only (no premium).

---

**Full ratchet anti-dilution.** Punitive on down rounds. Resets the conversion price to the new round price regardless of size.

**Counter:** Broad-based weighted average is standard.

## Control

**Pay-to-play with no thresholds.** Should require reasonable pro rata to avoid penalty conversion.

**Counter:** Pay-to-play only triggers for investors who don't participate in their full pro rata. Carve-outs for funds with concentration limits.

---

**Founder personal indemnification of investors.** Company indemnifies, not founders personally. Major red flag.

**Counter:** Strike entirely. Company-level indemnity only.

---

**Veto over hiring decisions / family hire vetoes / day-to-day operational vetoes.** Overly controlling. Not standard.

**Counter:** Protective provisions should be at the shareholder/class-vote level for fundamental changes (sale of company, dissolution, charter amendments). Operational vetoes belong at the Board level, not investor-by-investor.

---

**Monitoring fees / fee-for-service to investor.** PE-style. Not venture standard.

**Counter:** Strike. Venture investors don't charge monitoring fees.

---

**Drag-along without dragged-investor protections.** Should have customary carve-outs.

**Counter:** Drag-along requires majority of preferred + majority of common. Dragged investors get limited reps, capped liability, and same-form consideration.

## Process

**No-shop over 60 days.** Standard is 30-45 days.

**Counter:** 30-day no-shop, with break fee only if the company shops in violation.

---

**MFN-only SAFEs with no cap.** Investor gets nothing if the company stays at the same valuation forever.

**Counter:** Always include a valuation cap. MFN-only without a cap is unfair to early money.

---

**Stacking SAFEs with no clear conversion mechanics.** If the company has $2M+ in unconverted SAFEs across multiple caps, the cap table is a mess.

**Counter:** Set a SAFE total cap and require a priced round trigger by a specific date or AUM threshold.

## Cross-border (always check on Canada/US deals)

**Delaware governing law on a Canadian entity.** Doesn't translate well — Canadian corporate law applies regardless. Creates dispute uncertainty.

**Counter:** Governing law should match entity jurisdiction. Ontario for OBCA companies, Delaware for Delaware Cs.

---

**Missing Canadian hold-period legend.** Required by NI 45-106. 4 months + 1 day for accredited.

**Counter:** Add the prescribed legend. Standard CVCA form has it.

---

**Missing dual securities law reps.** If the deal spans Canada and the US, both NI 45-106 and Reg D Rule 506 reps need to be in the SPA.

**Counter:** Add cross-border rep section.

---

**Missing private issuer confirmation.** For Canadian private issuers, this matters for distribution rules.

**Counter:** Add the rep.

---

**US-style provisions that don't translate.** S-1 registration rights for a Canadian entity, for example.

**Counter:** Strike or convert to Canadian equivalents (typically demand registration rights triggered on a Canadian IPO, not S-1).

---

**Foreign control structures that jeopardize CCPC status.** Anything that gives non-residents board majority or operational veto can blow up CCPC. Massive SR&ED / small business deduction implications for the company.

**Counter:** Investor seats are fine. Investor majority of the board is not. Investor operational vetoes that effectively give non-resident control are not.

## Employee / founder

**Blanket non-competes (Ontario employees).** Largely unenforceable in Ontario as of 2021. Burns goodwill.

**Counter:** Narrow to C-suite with reasonable scope and duration (typically 12 months, defined competitive set).

## How to use this list

For any term sheet or SAFE review:

1. Scan for every item above. Yes / no for each.
2. For each "yes," name it, quote the exact clause, explain the dollar/control harm, propose specific replacement language.
3. Assign a traffic light (RED / YELLOW / GREEN) based on how aggressive the provision is in the context of the rest of the deal.
4. Lead the analysis with the worst red flag.
